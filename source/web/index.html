What it actually does

Not blind upload first. Library control first.

The app does not try to mount your disk in the browser. It uses a local connector to read approved folders, pushes clip metadata into the cloud API, and gives you a subscription-ready search and assistant experience on top.

Trust boundary

What syncs, what stays local.

Synced to the cloud

  • clip titles, categories, characters, and paths
  • duration, size, aspect ratio, and cleaned status
  • devices, roots, plans, sessions, and account state

Stays on the machine

  • the rest of the local filesystem
  • folders you do not explicitly approve
  • full video uploads unless you add that feature later

Why this matters

This is why the product can be sold safely. The browser never gets direct folder access. The connector is the filesystem boundary, and the API is the contract.

Migration note

How to bring old Sora exports into iLL Motion.

If you already downloaded your Sora history, you do not need a special migration wizard. Point the connector at those export folders and iLL Motion will index them like any other local archive.

  1. Keep your Sora folders where they are, such as D:\SORA or D:\sora no watermarks.
  2. Create an account here so the connector has an owner account to attach to.
  3. Run the connector against those folders from your machine.
  4. Let the metadata sync, then search the archive online by prompt, category, cleaned status, or folder type.

What comes through

  • titles and filenames
  • categories like remixes, profiles, and cleaned outputs
  • durations, sizes, and aspect ratios
  • a searchable cloud catalog built from your local export
